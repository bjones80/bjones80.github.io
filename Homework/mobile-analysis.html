Mobile Browsing - Analysis

Introduction

I used my phone to browse web pages.

Questions to consider:

  • Can you still see what you want to see?
  • Can you find what you're looking for?
  • Can you do what you want to do?
  • Any frustrations?
  • Anything work better on the small screen?

Findings

Can you still see what you want to see?

Most of the time you can, there are times that it doesn't work as well. The biggest web page that I had problems with was I-Learn. It was hard to see what I needed to see.

Can you find what you're looking for?

Most of the time I could see what I wanted. There was time that I had to search around and find what I needed. Again, the biggest web page I problems with is I-Learn.

Can you do what you want to do?

Yes, I often found that you can get where you want to go faster on the mobile. It is simplified and I really like that

Any frustrations?

Yes, there are times that you couldn't click on what you need to, it would often give me a slight head ache to try to navigate on some web page.

Anything work better on the small screen?

The good web pages had good form and better ways to get around the page.

Summary

What did you learn? Where there any surprises? I found that there were a lot of differences, I often would get mad because I couldn't click on what I wanted to. There are times that the mobile was better but often I would rather go with the desktop.
